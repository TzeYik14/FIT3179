COVID-19 Average Cases by Continent



By calculating the average number of cases over a specified period, we can understand the relative impact of the pandemic on different continents.


The high average COVID-19 cases in South America can be attributed to a combination of demographic, healthcare, socioeconomic, and behavioral factors. The continent's densely populated urban centers, where people often live in close quarters, have facilitated the rapid spread of the virus.

Oceania, encompassing various Pacific countries and territories, has consistently maintained a lower average of COVID-19 cases compared to many other regions. This can be attributed to a combination of geographical isolation, stringent border controls, early implementation of precautionary measures, effective public health responses, relatively low population density, and lessons learned from past outbreaks.
