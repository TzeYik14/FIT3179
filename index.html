Suicide Rate vs. Depression Rate: Population-Scaled Analysis



Visualizing the correlation between suicide rates and depression rates provides valuable insights into the mental health landscape. It helps identify patterns, disparities, and potential risk factors, enabling policymakers, healthcare professionals, and researchers to make informed decisions.

The high depression rate in India caused a high suicide rate as depression can lead to suicide due to overwhelming emotional pain, hopelessness, isolation, and impaired problem-solving abilities, creating a desperate state.

The prevalnce of depression disorder in males is more than females. Gender roles, societal expectations and stigma around mental health affect both men and women.

Suicide Rate by Gender



Research has consistently shown that men tend to have a higher suicide rate as compared to women

Men may experience societal pressure to suppress emotions, seek help less frequently, and choose more lethal means, contributing to higher suicide rates. Gender norms often discourage emotional vulnerability, leading to untreated mental health issues.
